Our Story

Where Every
Meal Tells a
Story

"Eat Pham" — in Manipuri, it simply means a place to eat. Since 2015, we have been filling one of Delhi's most significant culinary gaps: a warm, honest, deeply authentic window into the North-Eastern heart of India.

Tucked into the vibrant lanes of Humayunpur in Safdarjung Enclave, Eatpham is one of the very few restaurants in the capital serving genuine Manipuri food — the kind that carries the perfume of ngari, the heat of umorok, and the comfort of hand-steamed rice.

Our kitchen honours centuries of Meitei culinary tradition — minimal oil, maximum flavour. Every dish is prepared with care, using time-honoured techniques that celebrate the purity of each ingredient.

The Manipuri Way

A Philosophy of Purity

Light, clean, and deeply nourishing — Manipuri cuisine is one of India's best-kept culinary secrets.

Minimal Oil, Maximum Soul

Manipuri cooking shuns heavy oils. Ingredients are boiled, steamed, or lightly sautéed — letting natural flavours speak for themselves in every bowl.

Ngari — The Sacred Ferment

Fermented freshwater fish, matured for months in earthen pots, forms the umami backbone of Manipuri cuisine — an ingredient as essential as salt itself.

Umorok — King of Chillies

One of the world's hottest chillies, grown in the hills of Northeast India. It lends Manipuri cuisine its characteristic fire and unforgettable depth.

Seasonal & Indigenous

Bamboo shoots, water spinach, black rice, lotus stems, and rare hill greens — our menu follows the rhythm of the seasons and the bounty of the Northeast.

Community & Celebration

Food is identity in Manipur. Every dish at Eatpham carries the warmth of festivals like Cheiraoba and the tradition of families gathering around a shared thali.

Nourishment First

Manipuri cooking is inherently healthy — light broths, steamed vegetables, and fermented probiotics make every meal as good for the body as it is for the soul.

Sourced from the Hills

Ingredients That
Travel Far

Manipur is an emerald jewel in India's far northeast — a land of rolling hills, pristine rivers, and rare biodiversity. Our ingredients carry the essence of that landscape to your table in Delhi.

We source our ngari (fermented fish), umorok (king chilli), black rice, bamboo shoots, and indigenous herbs directly from Manipur, ensuring every dish is as authentic as it would be in Imphal itself.

When you eat at Eatpham, you're not just dining — you're taking a journey to one of India's most remarkable, least-explored culinary cultures.
